# 2026-06-08 — Diameter at lr=4e-6: the explore-vs-stability tension (beta sweep)

Context: the diameter task is **already solved at lr=2e-6** (see 2026-06-07 — `rl-diameter-mw4`:
physics 0.476, n→6, stable, max entropy 0.45, no divergence). This doc is a *speed-optimization*
sub-investigation: can we keep the 3-4× faster **lr=4e-6** recipe (the gravity sweep winner) for
the diameter task? lr=4e-6 turns out to be too hot for the longer horizon-dip completions, and the
two beta endpoints bracket the failure cleanly.

## Runs (all: diameter target, noise 0.08, 4 worlds/step, lr 4e-6, 250 steps)

| run | beta | n_experiments | accuracy (physics) | outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `rl-diameter-mw4-lr4e6` | 0.005 | climbs 1 → ~12 | peak **0.68** (~step 134), eroded to ~0.45 | **learns to aggregate, then entropy runaway ~step 160** |
| `rl-diameter-mw4-beta01` | 0.01 | **stuck at 1.0** (0% ≥3) | flat ~**0.29** | **stable but OVER-ANCHORED — never explores aggregation** |
| `rl-diameter-mw4-beta0075` | 0.0075 | climbs 1 → ~10 (median 9) | **0.60** (late third), no erosion | **WINNER — aggregates AND stays stable through 250 steps** |

### beta=0.005 @ lr=4e-6 — learns then diverges
Aggregation emerged fast (n_exp → ~12, well above the ~7 needed to beat 8% noise) and accuracy
peaked ~0.68. Then a sustained, accelerating entropy runaway from ~step 150:
`entropy 0.55→0.70→0.86→1.06→1.33→1.68`, with `grad_norm 0.6→1.9` and `kl 0.56→0.91` climbing in
lockstep, accuracy eroding off the peak. Stopped at ~step 170 (no checkpoint lost — `--no-save-final`).
The higher lr + the diameter task's long completions (call-freq ~12 vs gravity's ~6) overwhelm the
beta=0.005 KL anchor. (Note: at lr=2e-6 the *same* beta=0.005 is stable — 2026-06-07.)

### beta=0.01 @ lr=4e-6 — stable but frozen
No runaway (entropy ~0.4 throughout), but the KL anchor is now strong enough to pin the policy at
the base model's one-shot behavior: **n_experiments stuck at exactly 1.0 (100% of rollouts), 0% do
≥3, measurement reward exactly 0.000 the whole run.** It never discovers that averaging multiple
noisy measurements beats the noise. Accuracy flat ~0.29 (≈ single-measurement quality). This is the
documented "0.02 over-anchors" failure, showing here at 0.01 for this task/lr.

### beta=0.0075 @ lr=4e-6 — WINS (250 steps, exit 0, no divergence)
Threads the needle: accuracy climbs **monotonically** 0.228 → 0.499 → 0.598 (early/mid/late thirds;
no erosion — the last 12 steps average ~0.62), n_experiments → ~10 (median 9, 99.5% ≥3), measurement
0.123 → 0.403. The watchdog reported FINISHED (not divergence); `frac_reward_zero_std` stayed 0 (clean
GRPO signal). Entropy crept 0.31 → 0.41 → **0.574** and grad_norm held ~0.67 — elevated but a slow
creep, not the explosive 0.55→1.68-in-12-steps runaway of beta=0.005.

**This beats the proven recipe on both axes**: faster (lr 4e-6 vs 2e-6) AND better (accuracy 0.60 vs
the lr=2e-6 run's 0.476; n≈10 vs n≈6).

## Read
A real **explore-vs-stability tension at lr=4e-6**: beta=0.005 explores aggregation but entropy runs
away; beta=0.01 is stable but freezes the policy before it explores; **beta=0.0075 sits in the sweet
spot** — explores AND stays stable to 250. New diameter recipe: **lr=4e-6 / beta=0.0075 / 4 worlds-step**.

**One open watch-item**: at beta=0.0075 entropy is still gently rising at step 250 (0.41→0.57 over the
late third). Whether it plateaus or eventually destabilizes past 250 is untested — a longer-horizon
confirmation run is the natural de-risk before adopting this as THE recipe for the bigger builds (spin,
mixed-target). Conservative fallback remains the rock-stable lr=2e-6 / beta=0.005 (max entropy 0.45).
